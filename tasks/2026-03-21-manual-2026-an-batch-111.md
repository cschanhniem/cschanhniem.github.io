## Editorial Notes

- `an4.31-34` were kept as a coherent doctrinal cluster around fourfold supports:
  - conditions for prosperity
  - four saṅgahavatthu
  - the Buddha’s lion roar in relation to devas
  - supreme forms of confidence
- `an4.35-38` are individual discourses and were written with full argumentative bodies:
  - `Vassakāra` pivots from worldly intelligence to liberative greatness
  - `Doṇa` must preserve the lotus image and the negative answers without flattening them into a slogan
  - `Non-decline` keeps the full training sequence
  - `Withdrawn` preserves the four-part definition and the doctrinal list of speculative views
- `an4.39-40` were kept as two separate sacrifice discourses. They overlap, but `Udāyī` shifts the weight onto timely, non-violent, well-directed offering.
