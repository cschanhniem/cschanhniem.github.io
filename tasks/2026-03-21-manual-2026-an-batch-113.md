## Editorial Notes

- an4.51 and an4.52 are the two overflowing merit routes that open the vagga and establish different axes of merit: an4.51 is merit via requisites donated to contemplatives; an4.52 is merit via the four Jewels of Refuge (internal) — keep them distinct, not summarized as one type.
- an4.53 and an4.54 are deliberately a paired audience: same zombie/god schema, but 53 is told to laity on a road (with 5-sīla frame), and 54 is told to monastics (with 10-kamma-patha frame that adds wrong speech, covetousness, ill-will, wrong view). Do not collapse the two.
- an4.53's kệ has **six stanzas** (not four); an4.54 uses the same six stanzas (peyyāla from 53). Both were kept full.
- an4.55 has the beloved Nakula parents' narrative frame — preserve the direct speech of both husband and wife.
- an4.56 is the shorter monastic version of the same teaching — keep as distinct route, not merged with 55.
- an4.57, 58, 59 form a food-giving triad: female noble disciple (Suppavāsā), male lay benefactor (Anāthapiṇḍika), generic (anyone). Note different verse in 57 vs 58-59.
- an4.60 closes the vagga by tying an4.51's requisites-to-meditators teaching back to lay practice as a whole: cúng dường tứ vật dụng = hành trì đúng đắn của người tại gia.
