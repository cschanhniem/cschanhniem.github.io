## Editorial Notes
- **an4.111 (Kesi)**: famous sutta recounting the Buddha teaching Kesi the horse trainer. The word for "kill them" is kept robustly (`Ta giết vị ấy`), which is then immediately contextualized as "doesn't think they're worth advising or instructing".
- **an4.112 (Speed)**: 4 factors of a fine thoroughbred (ajjava, java, khanti, soracca) — translated as sự ngay thẳng, tốc độ, sự kham nhẫn, và sự hiền hòa.
- **an4.113 (The Goad)**: The 4 goad levels (shadow, hairs, hide, bone) mapped to varying degrees of proximity to suffering/impermanence.
- **an4.114 (Royal Elephant)**: 4 factors (listens, destroys, endures, goes fast) — nghe, tiêu diệt (dục, sân, ác pháp), kham nhẫn (lạnh, nóng, mắng chửi), đi nhanh (tới Niết-bàn).
- **an4.115 (Things)**: A complex 4-way conditional matrix about pleasant/unpleasant tasks and beneficial/harmful outcomes. Emphasized the contrast between the fool (who only considers pleasure) and the astute (who prioritizes benefit).
- **an4.116 (Diligence)**: 4 places for diligence: replacing bad bodily, verbal, and mental conduct with good ones, and wrong view with right view.
- **an4.117 (Guarding)**: Protecting the mind from 4 affective triggers: `rajanīyesu dhammesu` (pháp sinh lòng luyến ái), dosanīyesu (sân hận), mohanīyesu (si mê), madanīyesu (kiêu ngạo/đe dọa/say nghiện).
- **an4.118 (Inspiring)**: 4 places of pilgrimage (Lumbinī, Bodhgayā, Sārnāth, Kusinārā).
- **an4.119 (Perils (1st))**: 4 perils: rebirth, old age, sickness, death.
- **an4.120 (Perils (2nd))**: 4 external perils: fire, water, kings, bandits. Includes the `uddāna` at the end marking the vagga conclusion.
