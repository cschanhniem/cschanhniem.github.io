## Summary

This tranche opens `AN 4` with the first five discourses of the `Bhaṇḍagāmavagga`. Unlike the `Rāgapeyyāla` matrix just completed, this block shifts back into full individual suttas, so each route had to be authored as a distinct discourse rather than as a templated doctrinal slice.

## Editorial Notes

- `an4.1` keeps the four noble factors, ethics, immersion, wisdom, and freedom, in a clean ascending cadence and preserves the closing verse as a compact seal.
- `an4.2` was written as a stark doctrinal pair, fallen and not fallen, without rhetorical padding.
- `an4.3` preserves the ethical and epistemic force of praise, blame, trust, and mistrust, and keeps the warning verse sharp rather than schematic.
- `an4.4` keeps the four honored relationships explicit, mother, father, the Tathāgata, and the Tathāgata’s disciples.
- `an4.5` was treated as a four-person map of practice, not as a loose list. The prose keeps the movement from drifting with craving to standing firm and finally crossing beyond.
