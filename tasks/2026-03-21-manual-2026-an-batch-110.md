## Editorial Notes

- `an4.21-24` are not peyyāla. They need full argumentative bodies:
  - reverence for Dhamma after awakening
  - true seniority by qualities, not age
  - Tathāgata and the world
  - Kāḷaka epistemology with non-conceiving
- `an4.25` was kept lean but not clipped. The center of gravity is the purpose of the holy life: restraint, relinquishment, fading, cessation.
- `an4.26` was corrected toward the English doctrinal shape where the Minh Châu shell drifts. The paired contrast is:
  - deceitful, flattering, pompous, fake, insolent, scattered
  - genuine, not flattering, attentive, amenable, serene
- `an4.27-30` were written as a coherent ascetic-contentment cluster:
  - trifling requisites
  - noble traditions
  - footprints of the Dhamma
  - defense of those footprints before wanderers
