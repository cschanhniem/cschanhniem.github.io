## Editorial Notes
- **an4.101-105**: The first half of the vagga applies five metaphors (clouds, clouds again, pots, lakes, mangoes) to four types of individuals. The first two contrast "talking/memorizing Dhamma" with "acting/understanding the Four Noble Truths". The next three contrast "impressive outward deportment" with "understanding the Four Noble Truths". The translations rigorously preserve the identical matrix logic across all variations.
- **an4.106**: A ghost sutta (no text in any SC edition). A brief explanatory placeholder file was created to keep the indexing contiguous and match the uddāna verse at the end of the vagga, which counts 10 suttas including two mango suttas. 
- **an4.107**: The Mice simile. Applied to making a hole (memorizing) vs living in it (understanding).
- **an4.108**: The Oxen simile. Applied to intimidating one's own followers vs the followers of others. Kept the translation of `balībadda` (bò đực lực lưỡng) consistent.
- **an4.109**: The Trees simile. Applied to the character of a person (hardwood/softwood) vs their followers (hardwoods/softwoods). `pheggu` translates to `gỗ xốp`, `sāro` translates to `gỗ lõi`.
- **an4.110**: The Vipers simile. Applied to how fast anger arises (fast-acting venom) vs how long it lingers (lethal venom). Ends the vagga.
