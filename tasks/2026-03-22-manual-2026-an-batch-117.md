## Editorial Notes
- **an4.91**: Asura/deva combinations mapped to leading and following in unethical/ethical conduct. Maintained clear parallel structure.
- **an4.92-94**: The three samādhi suttas (Immersion). They systematically build on the combinations of `chỉ tịnh` (samatha / serenity) and `tăng thượng tuệ pháp quán` (vipassanā / discernment). In an4.93, used the striking simile of clothes/head on fire for the person lacking both. In an4.94, carefully structured the specific questions one should ask a teacher.
- **an4.95**: Chavālātasutta (A Firebrand). Famous simile of the dung-smeared burning log from a funeral pyre. Translated clearly to contrast the four types of practicing for self vs others.
- **an4.96-99**: Applied the "self vs others" matrix to: removing defilements (an4.96), learning/teaching aptitude (an4.97), a skeleton frame (an4.98), and the five precepts (an4.99). Kept the phrasing consistent across the matrix.
- **an4.100**: Potaliyasutta. A narrative ending the vagga. Contrasted equanimity (upekkhā) with the Buddha's standard of "understanding time and context" (kālaññutā) for praising/criticizing. Included the standard lay follower refuge formula at the end.
