## Summary

This tranche completes the rest of `AN 4.1-10`. The editorial challenge here is different from the long serial peyyāla work that came before. `an4.6-10` are short to medium discourses, but each one has a distinct internal logic and should stand as a full sutta, not as a doctrinal note or compressed paraphrase.

## Editorial Notes

- `an4.6` was written as a fourfold contrast between little and much learning, then between missing and grasping the point of learning. The key was to preserve that the real dividing line is not quantity alone, but understanding and living the Dhamma.
- `an4.7` was written as a communal discourse, not a generic praise text. The monk, nun, layman, and laywoman each remain visible as four supports that make the Saṅgha shine.
- `an4.8` keeps the doctrinal weight of the Buddha’s four self-assurances. The translation preserves the bull’s place, the lion’s roar, and the turning of the wheel as public declarations grounded in realization.
- `an4.9` stays lean and exact. The four triggers for craving remain concrete, robes, almsfood, lodgings, and longing for existence, while the verse keeps craving as the wandering companion through saṁsāra.
- `an4.10` had to stay structurally complete. The translation preserves both halves, the four yokes and the four unyokings, and keeps the fivefold analytic frame, origin, ending, gratification, danger, and escape, intact for each domain.
